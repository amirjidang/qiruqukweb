What Is Lionel Richie Known For?

Lionel Richie got his start in the entertainment business during the height of soul music. In 1968, when Richie co-founded the Commodores, Motown Records was one of the most prolific music labels in the world.

Known for acts such as the Supremes, Temptations, Smokey Robinson and the Miracles, Four Tops, and Jackson 5, Motown created a sound, that blended soul and pop, which appealed to a universal audience.

Richie was a student at Tuskegee Institute when he met his fellow Commodores members. After a brief and unsuccessful signing with Atlantic Records, the group signed with Motown. During their early days at the label, they supported the Jackson 5 and were their opening act during concerts.

The Commodores released multiple successful albums with Motown, and had massive hits with songs such as "Easy", "Brick House", "Three Times a Lady", and "Nightshift." They also appeared in two films, Scott Joplin and Thank God Its Friday.

Richie gained notoriety as the group's lead singer and eventually decided to pursue a solo career after he left the Commodores in 1982. In 1981, Richie recorded "Endless Love", a duet with Diana Ross. The song received commercial and critical acclaim and has become one of the most beloved love songs of all time.

His debut album, which was self-titled, came out in October 1982 and introduced staples such as "My Love", "You Are", and "Truly." "Can't Slow Down" followed in 1983 and featured songs such as "All Night Long (All Night)" and "Hello". The album, which cemented Richie's solo success, even won a Grammy for Album of the Year. "Say You, Say Me", which Richie recorded for the 1985 film White Nights, won the Academy Award for Original Song in 1986.

While Richie was enjoying great success during the beginning of his solo career, he was also the subject of plagiarism lawsuits. In 1985, songwriters Gene Thompson and Tracey Singleton sued Richie as they believed some of his hit records were too similar to compositions they wrote in 1979. Fortunately for Richie, the judge for the case did not see any merit in Thompson and Singleton's claims.

"Dancing on the Ceiling", Richie's third album and last album of the decade, was released in August 1986. It featured songs such as "Love Will Conquer All", "Ballerina Girl", and the title track.

Richie continued to record music and release albums throughout the 1990s and 2000s, earning a reputation as one of the most romantic, soulful voices of the era. Many of his songs became Billboard Top 100 hits. He eventually became a judge on American Idol in 2018, joining Katy Perry and Luke Bryan. Besides being a judge on American Idol, Richie has made appearances on Jeopardy and American Housewife.
