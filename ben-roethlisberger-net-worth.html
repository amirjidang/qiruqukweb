Early Life and Career Highlights

Ben Roethlisberger, born on March 2, 1982, in Lima, Ohio, is a former American National Football League (NFL) quarterback. He attended Miami University, where he had an exceptional college football career, breaking numerous records and earning multiple accolades.

In the 2004 NFL Draft, Roethlisberger was selected as the 11th overall pick by the Pittsburgh Steelers, marking the beginning of his professional career. He quickly established himself as a talented and efficient quarterback, showcasing his skills on the field.

Throughout his career, Roethlisberger achieved remarkable success and garnered recognition for his exceptional performances. In his first season, he won the NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year award, setting the stage for future accomplishments.

Roethlisberger’s career highlights include leading the Steelers to two Super Bowl victories and being selected to the Pro Bowl seven times. His leadership and playmaking abilities contributed significantly to the success of his team.
