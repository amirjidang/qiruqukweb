How Did Dakota Fanning Launch Her Little Sister Elle Fanning's Career?

As of the time of this writing, Dakota Fanning and Elle Fanning have both been acting in major projects for more than two decades. Since the sisters have been successful for so long, it can be easy to forget what launched their careers, to begin with.

In the year 1999, Dakota Fanning's professional acting career began when she starred in a Tide commercial at only five years old. The following year, Dakota Fanning's career went to a whole new level when she landed roles in several major TV shows like ER, Spin City, Ally McBeal, and CSI: Crime Scene Investigation.

After turning heads with her performances in those shows, Dakota Fanning landed one of the lead roles in a movie with Sean Penn. When that movie which was called I Am Sam was released, it became extremely acclaimed with Dakota Fanning receiving a huge portion of the praise.

After the success of I Am Sam, Dakota Fanning went on to star in the TV miniseries Taken. Landing a notable role in that miniseries was a big deal for Dakota Fanning since it was produced by Steven Spielberg.

When Dakota Fanning began working with Penn and Spielberg, she brought her little sister along for the ride. The reason for that is the first two roles Elle Fanning landed was as a younger version of Dakota Fanning's character in I Am Sam and Taken.

When Dakota Fanning and Elle Fanning were still kids, they became such talented actors that everyone in Hollywood wanted to work with them. Between the sisters, they worked with heavyweights like Denzel Washington, Tom Cruise, Eddie Murphy, Sean Penn, Jeff Bridges, and many more.

Since the Fanning sisters were so great in many movies, it is very safe to say that they both were talented actors as kids. With that in mind, it is safe to say that if they wanted to put up a front in public, the sisters were more than capable of playing their roles.
