Lindsay Lohan's Net Worth Used To Be $30 Million... Here's What She's Worth Now

Highlights

  • Lindsay Lohan's net worth was once $30 million, but it quickly diminished due to partying and debt.
  • Lohan had a successful acting career, earning millions from films and her album sales.
  • Lohan faced financial troubles and had to repay debts, resulting in her net worth dropping to $100,000. However, she has made quite a comeback, and her net worth is now $2 million.

Many used to deem Lindsay Lohan to be one of the most beautiful and amazing women of her generation. She managed to climb the ladder of success as a child actor and transition into meatier roles in her late teens, gathering love and support from fans all around the world.

However, the desire to be out and partying with friends as a young adult began to take a hit on the success that was Lohan's career. As such, her net worth, which was at one time a staggering $30 million, quickly began to diminish. Not only were roles no longer coming in, but Lohan had a number of debts that she was ordered to repay.

Because of this, Lohan's net worth went down to the low six figures. But, over the years, Lohan has turned her life around and has been able to rebuild her net worth to $2 million today.

Lindsay Lohan's Lucrative Television And Film Career

Lohan earned $30 million over the course of her acting career

After the release of Mean Girls in 2004, The Parent Trap actress' fortune began to skyrocket. Lohan appeared on The Masked Singer Australia as well and her income soared from $1 million to a whopping $7.5 million.

She earned more than $20 million by acting in several successful movies, such as TheCanyons (2013), Freaky Friday (2003), Confessions of a Teenage Drama Queen (2004), Liz and Dick (2012), Just My Luck (2006) and Georgia Rule (2007).

Lindsay Lohan Released An Album

Lohan sold one million copies of her debut album

While not all critics were impressed with Lohan's album, they did not pan it either.

Lindsay Lohan Launched A Fashion Line

Lohan's brand, 6126, was named to pay homage to Marilyn Monroe

In 2008, Lohan launched her own fashion line called 6126. Something that was a fad for starlets in Hollywood to do at the time.

According to CNN, the brand was named after the birthday of Lohan's idol, Marilyn Monroe. Monroe's birthday was June 1, 1926.

Lindsay Lohan's Other Profitable Ventures

Lohan made millions by appearing on The Oprah Winfrey Show

Lohan was on the cover of the January-February 2012 issue of Playboy. While the move was deemed controversial, Lohan earned $1 million for the spot.

In August 2013, Lohan appeared with Oprah Winfrey on The Oprah Winfrey Show. While Winfrey is not known for paying her guests, for several interviews, Lohan earned $2 million.

However, Lohan's success would begin to fizzle around the mid-2000s. Instead of making money and being the belle of Hollywood, Lohan began to spiral. And when this happened, her net worth began to tank.

How Lindsay Lohan Lost Her Fortune

Lohan tried to keep up with others and partying got the best of her and her career

In 2007, Lohan was targeted by the paparazzi who followed her and captured every drunken night she had spent at the nightclubs. The actress who was 21 years old at the time was later admitted to the hospital during the shooting of Georgia Rule.

"She was overheated and dehydrated," Lohan's publicist said at the time. But, eventually, the truth came out that Lohan was just unable to make it to set as a result of staying out too late and partying.

James G. Robinson, who is a studio executive, made a public statement about Lohan.

The statement read that Lohan was "irresponsible and unprofessional." He also mentioned several other things like, "various late arrivals and absences from the set," and, "we are well aware that your ongoing all-night heavy partying is the real reason for your so-called 'exhaustion'."

Lohan's fame dropped rather drastically at that time and she was labeled as an insurance liability. It must have been so difficult for her and yet, she didn't want to give up so soon.

Lohan was given small projects and had been cast in the TV series Ugly Betty (2006) as a minor role. Additionally, she hosted a documentary about human trafficking in India for BBC.

But just as everyone has their good sides and bad, people apparently have focused on Lohan's bad side more often.

Being a woman of wealth at such an early age, she pampered herself by buying expensive cars, handbags, and designer clothes, and enjoyed meals at extravagant restaurants. Lohan even sometimes partied at three clubs in one single night.

Unfortunately, Lohan was also allegedly found using illicit drugs. In 2010, according to a Business Insider article, Hollyscoopreported on how much Lohan had spent on narcotics.

"There were reports a few days ago that Lohan was allegedly spending about $5,000 a week on drugs, but our 'source' insists that this figure is reportedly closer to about $3,500," the article stated. "The last few months Lohan was allegedly 'spotted' narcotics because she's in debt to the people who are supplying her, but no one's really giving her a hard time because she's Lindsay Lohan. The source added that Lohan is apparently in $30,000 debt for narcotics."

The story doesn't end there. Between 2007 and 2013, Lohan was arrested a few too many times for DUIs, altercations, shoplifting, and reckless driving.

In 2010, she was sentenced to 90 days in jail for not attending her court-ordered weekly education classes. Just as her appearances in court racked up, mugshots and rehab stints began to put her bank account into jeopardy.

Lindsay Lohan Has To Repay Her Debts

Lohan's bank account was seized by the IRS to repay debts

Lohan had managed to rack up hundreds of thousands of dollars of debt over the years. While she was able to push back paying it off for a time, eventually, she was ordered to pay it all back.

The bills that Lohan reportedly had included:

  • $61,000 rehab bill
  • $40,000 to a Nevada tanning salon
  • $233,000 in unpaid federal back taxes
  • $90,000 in unpaid limousine fees

In the end, the IRS seized her bank account to recoup the funds.

Lohan's net worth dropped dramatically from $30 million to $100,000. The money which she had earned on The Oprah Winfrey Show was used to pay back taxes, IRS debts, and rehab fees.

In October 2010, the 33-year-old star had an interview with Vanity Fair where she shared her tough times and financial issues.

"I was 18, 19--with a ton of money and no one really here to tell me that I couldn't do certain things," Lohan explained. "And I see where that's gotten me now, and I don't like it."

Lohan went on to say, "I would look up to those girls...The Britneys and whatever, and I would like, 'I want to be like that.'"
