Lizzie Borden Crime Scene Photos: A Grisly Discovery

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  628 words  |  Kelle Repass

The Lizzie Borden crime scene photos of infamous murders reveal the horrifying aftermath of the tragic event that took place on August 4, 1892. 

The Borden home at 92 Second Street, Fall River, Massachusetts, became the site of a gruesome crime where Andrew and Abby Borden were brutally murdered. 

Andrew Borden was found lifeless in his sitting room, while Abby Borden’s body was discovered upstairs in a guest room. 

The shocking murders sparked speculation about the involvement of Lizzie Borden, their daughter, who was arrested but ultimately acquitted. 

However, the exact murder weapon remains unknown, as no conclusive evidence linking any of the found axes or hatchets to the crime.

The gruesome details: The autopsies on the dining room table

Contrary to the popular rhyme, which exaggerated the number of blows, it was determined that Abby Borden was struck 19 times and Andrew was struck 10 times. 

The sheer brutality of the attacks left both victims unrecognizable. 

Although Lizzie was initially suspected, the question of who actually killed the Bordens remains unanswered, adding to the enduring allure of the case.

Medical examinations revealed the devastating extent of the injuries inflicted upon Andrew and Abby Borden. 

Andrew’s face and skull bore the brunt of the vicious blows, with one strike splitting his lips, lower jaw bone, and nose. 

Abby suffered extensive damage to her skull and brain, with 11 blows penetrating the back of her head.

The chilling Lizzie Borden crime scene photos

Lizzie Borden crime scene photos offer a haunting glimpse into the gruesome aftermath of the infamous murders.

The crime scene was characterized by the shocking violence inflicted upon the victims.

Andrew’s face was unrecognizable, while Abby was found face down in a pool of blood. 

Furthermore, Abby’s injuries included multiple blows to her skull, resulting in severe brain damage. 

The blood spatter indicated that Andrew was killed after Abby, as his blood was still flowing when examined.

Law enforcement officials investigating the incident discovered a hatchet in the cellar.

At first, it appeared to be tainted with bloodstains, but subsequent tests revealed no traces of blood on the tool. 

Furthermore, animal hairs were identified on the hatchet, proving that it was not connected to the crime.

Alongside this discovery, blood-soaked rags were found in the cellar as well.

However, the link between these rags and the crime remained uncertain.

Lizzie Borden’s explanation that she used the rags for m*nstruation was accepted without further scrutiny.

Missing clues

Surprisingly, Lizzie Borden had little blood on her skin or clothing, aside from a small speck on her underskirt. 

This raised suspicions that the bucket of rags found in the cellar might have contained her blood-soaked clothing. 

However, the authorities never examined the bucket contents, leaving this theory unproven. 

Witnesses recalled seeing Lizzie burning a dress after the murders.

However, Lizzie claimed that old paint had ruined her dress, and she did not wear it on the day of the crime.

The Lizzie Borden crime scene photos and house

The Lizzie Borden House is a must-see attraction in Massachusetts that has captivated tourists for years.

Despite the fatal events that happened there a century ago, visitors are still drawn to the house.

The original furnishings might be gone, but the current owners have meticulously reconstructed the interior to match the house’s original design. 

Visitors will be pleased to see blood-stained rags and other possible murder weapons and be provided with a glimpse of the details surrounding the tragic episode.
