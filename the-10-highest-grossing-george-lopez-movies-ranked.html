The 10 Highest-Grossing George Lopez Movies, Ranked

From his early days as a stand-up comedian to his iconic sitcom, George Lopez has been entertaining audiences for decades. With his unique brand of humor and relatable stories, it's no wonder he's become one of America's most beloved comedians. Join us as we countdown the top 10 highest-grossing George Lopez movies which will take you through some of his most memorable big-screen performances. From heartwarming family comedies to action-packed adventures, there's something for everyone on this list which has been meticulously ranked based on their worldwide box office grosses. Without further ado, let's get started on our journey but get ready to laugh, cry, and maybe even learn a thing or two about the man behind the comedy.

Gnome Alone - 2017 ($5.4 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In this animated fantasy-comedy directed by Peter Lepeniotis, George Lopez voices the character of Zook, a wise-cracking garden gnome who helps a young girl named Chloe (voiced by Becky G) save her new home from evil creatures. With his signature humor and charm, Lopez brings a lighthearted and fun energy to the film, making Gnome Alone a great pick for a family movie night. Gnome Alone didn’t perform so well at the box office as it managed a worldwide gross of $5.4 million.

Balls of Fury - 2007 ($41.1 Million)

Via Flickr/Plex

George Lopez brings his A-game to this outrageous sports comedy directed by Ben Garant. In Balls of Fury, a movie that grossed $41.1 million at the worldwide box office, Lopez plays the role of FBI agent Ernie Rodriguez, who recruits former ping pong champion Randy Daytona (played by Dan Fogler) to infiltrate a high-stakes tournament. With its over-the-top humor and ridiculous plot, this film is a true underdog story that's sure to keep you laughing from start to finish.

The Spy Next Door - 2010 ($45.2 Million)

Via Flickr/Nowrunning

If you're in the mood for an action-packed comedy, look no further than The Spy Next Door, the hit movie that grossed over $45 million at the worldwide box office. Jackie Chan stars as a retired CIA agent who gets roped into babysitting his neighbor's kids, and George Lopez plays his fellow agent and best friend, Glaze. With their impeccable comedic timing and on-screen chemistry, Lopez and Chan deliver a hilarious and heartwarming performance that will leave you in stitches.

The Adventures of Sharkboy and Lavagirl - 2005 ($69.4 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Prepare to take a trip down memory lane with this classic family adventure directed by Robert Rodriguez. George Lopez takes on the role of Mr. Electric, the primary villain of the film, and his performance is truly electrifying. With his over-the-top antics and larger-than-life energy, Lopez brings a unique flair to this beloved childhood favorite that went on to gross over $69 million at the worldwide box office.

Beverly Hills Chihuahua - 2008 ($149.3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

If you're a dog lover, you won't want to miss this family-friendly comedy directed by Raja Gosnell that made a lot of waves, grossing $149.3 million at the box office worldwide. George Lopez voices Papi, a suave and streetwise Chihuahua who helps Chloe (voiced by Drew Barrymore) find her way back home after getting lost in Mexico. With his charm and charisma, Lopez brings Papi to life and steals the show in this heartwarming adventure.

Valentine's Day - 2010 ($216.5 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Fall in love with this star-studded romantic comedy directed by Garry Marshall. George Lopez plays Alphonso, a florist who helps the characters navigate the ups and downs of love on Valentine's Day. With his natural charisma and impeccable comedic timing, Lopez delivers a standout performance in this heartwarming and hilarious film which grossed $216.5 million at the worldwide box office.

The Smurfs 2 - 2013 ($348.5 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In this sequel to the first Smurfs film, George Lopez once again lends his voice to the character of Grouchy Smurf. With his sarcastic humor and grumpy demeanor, Grouchy provides a hilarious contrast to the rest of the cheerful Smurf characters and with Lopez's impeccable comedic timing, Grouchy becomes one of the most memorable characters in the film, making this a must-watch for Smurf fans of all ages. Although The Smurfs 2 isn't as much of a hit as the first, it managed an impressive worldwide box office gross of $348.5 million.

Rio - 2011 ($484.6 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Get ready to samba with this animated comedy adventure directed by Carlos Saldanha. George Lopez voices the character of Rafael, a wise-cracking toucan who helps Blu (voiced by Jesse Eisenberg) on his journey to save his species. With his signature wit and humor, Lopez brings Rafael to life and adds a level of fun and excitement to the film that's impossible to resist. Rio received rave reviews from critics and grossed a whopping worldwide box office gross of over $484 million.

Rio 2- 2014 ($500.1 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Get ready to dance to the beat of your own drum with this animated comedy adventure directed by Carlos Saldanha. George Lopez once again voices Rafael the toucan, and his infectious energy and a quick wit make Rafael one of the most beloved characters in the film. With its catchy soundtrack and colorful animation, Rio 2 is a sequel that's just as fun and entertaining as the original, grossing a whopping $500.1 million at the worldwide box office.

The Smurfs - 2011 ($563.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Join the Smurfs on their big-screen adventure with this live-action/animated comedy directed by Raja Gosnell. George Lopez lends his voice to Grouchy Smurf, the lovable character known for his grumpy personality and sarcastic quips. With Lopez's delivery, Grouchy becomes a fan-favorite character that audiences love to hate, making this film a must-watch for Smurf fans of all ages. The Smurfs was so loved by fans that it became a box-office hit, grossing well over $563 million worldwide.
