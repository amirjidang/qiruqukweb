Conviction and Imprisonment

In 1936, Lucky Luciano faced a legal battle that would ultimately result in his conviction and imprisonment. After an extensive investigation led by District Attorney Thomas E. Dewey, Luciano was found guilty of compulsory prostitution and running a prostitution racket. These charges stemmed from his involvement in the lucrative underground world of organized crime.

As a result of his conviction, Luciano was sentenced to serve a prison term of 30 to 50 years. However, his story took a surprising turn during World War II.

Ultimately, in 1946, after serving a reduced prison term as a result of his wartime contributions, Luciano was deported to Italy.
