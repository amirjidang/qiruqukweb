The Life and Career of Dick Wilson

Dick Wilson, born on July 30, 1916, in Preston, Lancashire, England, had a fascinating journey in the world of show business. His family moved to Hamilton, Ontario, Canada, during his childhood, where Wilson’s passion for entertainment began to take shape. Starting with a part-time job at CHML radio in Hamilton at the age of fifteen, Wilson soon discovered his talent for captivating audiences.

Wilson’s dedication to his craft led him to graduate from the Ontario College of Art & Design. For the next two decades, he showcased his comedic acrobatic dancing skills in vaudeville shows, leaving a lasting impression on audiences. In 1954, Wilson became an American citizen and decided to pursue a career in film and television acting in California.

“I aspire to bring laughter and joy to people’s lives through my performances. It’s a privilege to entertain and connect with audiences.”

Throughout his career, Wilson made notable appearances in television shows like Bewitched, Hogan’s Heroes, and The Bob Newhart Show. However, his most renowned role came in the form of Mr. Whipple in the Charmin commercials. Wilson’s talent for bringing humor and relatability to his characters made him a perfect fit for the role, which would define his career.

“The Charmin commercials allowed me to showcase my comedic timing and engage with audiences on a personal level. It was an unforgettable experience.”

As Mr. Whipple, Wilson featured in a staggering 504 commercials, earning a significant salary while working only a few days a year. His portrayal of the frustrated and lovable store manager endeared him to audiences and made the Charmin commercials iconic.

The Magic of Mr. Whipple

Mr. Whipple quickly became a household name, captivating viewers with his catchphrase “Please don’t squeeze the Charmin.” The relatable scenarios portrayed in the commercials, combined with Wilson’s comedic talent, resonated with consumers across the United States. The commercials showcased the irresistibly soft Charmin toilet paper while eliciting laughter from the audience.

Personal Life and Death

Dick Wilson, the beloved actor known for his role as Mr. George Whipple in the iconic Charmin toilet paper commercials, had a rich personal life outside of his successful career in advertising. Wilson was married to his wife Meg for over five decades, from 1950 until his passing in 2007. The couple had three children together, including their daughter Melanie Wilson, who followed in her father’s footsteps and became an actress.

On November 18, 2007, at the age of 91, Dick Wilson passed away due to natural causes. His death occurred at the Motion Picture and Television Country House and Hospital in Woodland Hills, California, a residential care facility for those in the entertainment industry.
