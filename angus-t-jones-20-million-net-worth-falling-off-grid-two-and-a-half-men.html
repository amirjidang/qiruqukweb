Angus T. Jones is a former child star. He was born in Austin, TX in 1993. Jones is best known for playing Jake Harper on the sitcom Two and a Half Men with Charlie Sheen, Jon Cryer, and later Ashton Kutcher.

Before he got his spot on the CBS show, Jones was a child actor who starred in five films, including See Spot Run, George of the Jungle 2, Simpatico, The Rookie, and Bringing Down The House.

Having starred in one of the most popular sitcoms of all time, Jones was set for money, reportedly earning himself a net worth of $20 million. Sadly, he quit the show and acting suddenly, and hit rock bottom pretty fast. After quitting acting, Angus T. Jones ventured into other businesses.

Jones has been absent from Hollywood since he stopped acting, and here's how Angus T. Jones went from having a $20 million net worth to falling off the grid.

4 Why Did Angus T. Jones Quit Acting?

Angus T. Jones quit acting because he found God

  • Jones' new-found religion did not sync up with his role on Two and a Half Men and Jones quit

Jones spent ten seasons as a main character on Two and a Half Men. He then became a guest star in Seasons 11 and 12 before leaving the show altogether. The reason why he left the show is largely because he became devoutly religious.

In 2012, Jones did an interview with The Forerunner Chronicles where he discussed his literal coming-to-God. He was going to three or four churches every Sunday until someone told him about a Seventh-day Adventist Church, and he was instantly hooked.

Because of his newfound faith, Jones didn't find the show appropriate anymore and started publicly slamming the sitcom. "You cannot be a true God-fearing person and be on a television show like that," he said in the interview.

The former actor encouraged fans to stop watching the show and "filling their heads with filth."

3 Angus T. Jones Started A Career In Business

Angus T. Jones attended college for a time and then went into business with Justin Combs and Kene Orjioke

  • Jones is no longer a part of the multimedia company he started with Combs and Orjioke

After leaving the show and acting for good, the George of the Jungle 2 star, had big dreams of creating "bible-based stories and further sharing the word of God," according to People. When that didn't pan out, Jones enrolled in college. He attended the University of Colorado at Boulder, where he took up environmental studies.

However, at the end of his freshmen year, Jones changed his major to Jewish studies. It is not clear if he graduated or earned a degree, but he was excited to live a normal life.

In 2016, he became the president of Tonite, a multimedia and event production company started by Justin Combs and Kene Orjioke.

2 Hitting Rock-Bottom For Angus T. Jones

Angus T. Jones left Hollywood after his controversial interview regarding Two and a Half Men

  • Jones was not able to find work after Two and a Half Men ended

After Jones' controversial interview, his career took a downward turn, and he was never cast in anything after that. That was when Angus T. Jones officially left Hollywood.

However, in 2016, he told People that he had stepped away from religion.

"I'm interested in seeing where I go without an organization putting a stamp of approval on if I'm good or bad or whatever," Jones told the publication. Since then, he has disappeared from the spotlight and lived a normal life.

  • Jones appeared on one episode of The Bookie

As of 2024, Angus T. Jones is single, and enjoying life. He is photographed in Los Angeles from time to time. Jones is nearly unrecognizable from how he looked as a child. In his most recent sighting in August 2023, Jones stepped out barefoot with a long beard, glasses, black beanie, khaki shorts, and a black t-shirt that read "SHOQUIP," referring to his home state of Texas, on it.

There is not much information on Jones' current job or what he is doing now, as Jones lives a very private life. However, Jones did come out of retirement in November 2023 to do a guest appearance on The Bookie as a favor to Chuck Lorre.

Jones is living off his $20 million and royalties from the show and seems healthy and happy.
