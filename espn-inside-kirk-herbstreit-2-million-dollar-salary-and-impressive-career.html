Herbstreit was a letter winner every year he played for the Buckeyes at Ohio State University from 1989 to 1993.

In 1992, he was named co-captain of the Ohio State Buckeyes, just as his father had been three decades before. He was also named team MVP.

He set a school record for pass completions (28) while on the team, which Troy Smith broke in 2006.

The Kirk Herbstreit National Kickoff Classic is named after Herbstreit, who earned a bachelor’s degree in business administration in 1993. The event “pits prep football powerhouses from across the country against high school teams from Ohio and Texas.”

The sports analyst has participated in a number of charity events, including the Make A Wish Foundation Ultimate Sports Auction and the Buckeye Cruise for Cancer.

The former athlete joined ESPN in 1996 and has since worked as a lead analyst on “College GameDay” as well as an analyst and sideline reporter for ABC’s Saturday college football games.

The analyst has been a regular contributor to the Columbus ESPN Radio affiliate 97.1 The Fan and wrote a column for “The Sporting News” titled “Inside The Game With Kirk Herbstreit.”
