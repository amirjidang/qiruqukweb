Phil Collins is a musician, singer, and songwriter who is widely known as one of the most iconic musicians of all time. His journey has led him to become one of the richest lead singers of all time. From his early days as a drummer in Genesis to his solo career and many impressive collaborations, he has consistently demonstrated his musical abilities and more.

9 Phil Collins' Early Life With Music

Phil Collins was born in the Chiswick neighborhood of London, England, on January 30, 1951. He became interested in music at a young age, and he was especially good at playing the drums. Collins started out in the music business as a child actor in the 1960s. He was in a number of TV shows and stage productions.

Collins joined the rock band Flaming Youth as their drummer in the late 1960s because he loved music so much. This was the start of his career as a professional musician. But his meteoric rise to fame and wealth began when he joined the progressive rock band Genesis as their drummer and later as their lead singer.

8 Phil Collins Joined Genesis As A Drummer

Phil Collins joined Genesis in 1970, initially as their drummer. The band had already established a dedicated following in the progressive rock scene, but their lives were about to change, and that included Phil Collins'.

In 1975, the lead singer, Peter Gabriel, left Genesis. So the band could continue on, Collins stepped up and took on the dual role of both drummer and lead vocalist. TBH nobody saw that coming, but what an incredible twist of fate it was.

Under Collins' leadership, Genesis underwent a creative shift, embracing a more pop-oriented sound. This transformation paid off handsomely, as the band went on to achieve success like never before with hit albums like A Trick of the Tail (1976) and Duke (1980). However, it was the album Invisible Touch (1986) that launched Genesis to global stardom.

Invisible Touch produced chart-topping hits like "Land of Confusion" and "In Too Deep" that are still played today and record-breaking sales. Stereogum says that Invisible Touch hit number one in the UK and number three on the US Billboard 200. It has sold more than 1.2 million copies in the UK and 6 million copies in the US, making it the band's best-selling album to date.

Collins' work as the singer, songwriter, and drummer for Genesis was by far the reason for the band's success. His unique voice and the lyrics in the music he wrote really connected with people, and it showed.

These albums featured a string of hit singles, such as "Sussudio," "One More Night," and "Another Day in Paradise." His ability to create emotion through his music is incredible. All of these songs are still known and most certainly contributed significantly to Phil Collins' net worth.

6 Phil Collins' Momentous Collaborations Paid Well

Phil Collins' financial success is due in part to how versatile he is as a musician and how willing he is to work with other artists from different genres. His work as a drummer has been in high demand; artists from Eric Clapton to Brand X have asked him to play drums on their songs and albums.
