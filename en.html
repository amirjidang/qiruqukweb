Biography

Loni Kaye Anderson (born August 5, 1945), known simply as Loni Anderson, is a prolific actress and TV personality from the United States. Her acting career has spanned over half a century. Born in 1945, Anderson made a name for herself with her outstanding portrayal of the bubbly receptionist Jennifer Marlowe on the CBS sitcom WKRP in Cincinnati, which aired from 1978 to 1982.

Her exceptional performance as the charming and clever Jennifer Marlowe won her three coveted Golden Globe Awards and two Emmy Awards nominations, solidifying her remarkable talent and skill as an actress. Loni Anderson‘s portrayal of the endearing character endeared her to fans worldwide, and she quickly became one of the most beloved and recognizable actresses of her time.

Loni Anderson‘s range as an actress was also on full display in her role as Jennifer Marlowe, as she masterfully portrayed the character’s complexity and depth, bringing her to life in a way that few other actresses could.

Early Life & Education

Loni Kaye Anderson is an American actress who has left an indelible mark in the entertainment industry. Born on August 5th, 1945, in Saint Paul, Minnesota, she is the daughter of Klaydon Carl “Andy” Anderson, an environmental chemist, and Maxine Hazel Kallin, a model. She spent most of her childhood in the suburban area of Roseville alongside her sister Andrea Sams, and in 1963, she was crowned Valentine Queen at Alexander Ramsey Senior High School‘s winter formal.

Interestingly, Loni Anderson‘s parents initially intended to name her “Leiloni,” but her father had a sudden change of heart, realizing that it could be twisted into “Lay Loni.” Therefore, they decided to shorten her name to just “Loni.”

Loni Anderson‘s career in acting began in the 1970s when she made guest appearances on various television shows before landing her breakthrough role as Jennifer Marlowe in the CBS sitcom WKRP in Cincinnati. Her witty and intelligent portrayal of the character earned her three Golden Globe Awards and two Emmy Awards nominations, solidifying her position as one of the most talented actresses in the industry.

Beyond her exceptional acting skills, Loni Anderson has also been recognized for her beauty, with her signature blonde hair and captivating smile capturing the hearts of many.

Personal Life

Loni Anderson‘s personal life has seen quite a few ups and downs. She has walked down the aisle four times with different partners. Her first marriage was with Bruce Hasselberg, which lasted from 1964 to 1966. Later on, she exchanged wedding vows with Ross Bickell in 1973, which ended in 1981. Then, she entered into matrimony with legendary actor Burt Reynolds in 1988, lasting until 1994.

But love knocked at her door again when she met Bob Flick, a famous musician of The Brothers Four fame, at a movie premiere way back in 1963. The couple tied the knot on May 17, 2008, and have been together ever since.

Loni Anderson is a proud mother of two children. Her first daughter, Deidra Hoffman, was born during her first marriage to Bruce Hasselberg. Later on, she adopted Quinton Anderson Reynolds with her former husband, Burt Reynolds. Not to forget, she also has a loving sister named Andrea Sams.

In 1995, Loni Anderson shared her life story in her autobiography, My Life in High Heels. The book is an engaging memoir that offers insights into the ups and downs of growing up with World War II parents who were both smokers.

It is also worth mentioning that Loni Anderson had a romantic relationship with actor Gary Sandy from 1981 to 1982.

Career

Loni Anderson, an actress of great merit and acclaim, marked her debut in the film industry in 1966 in the film Nevada Smith where she starred alongside the legendary Steve McQueen. Despite her impressive performance, she struggled to land significant roles for almost a decade. 

Her hard work and persistence paid off when she secured guest appearances on several popular TV shows, such as S.W.A.T., Phyllis, Police Woman, and Harry O in the mid-1970s, a time when television was the go-to source of entertainment for many.

Although she was not initially cast as Chrissy in Three’s Company (1978) as she had hoped, her exceptional performance at the audition attracted ABC’s attention, ultimately leading to her landing the iconic role of Jennifer Marlowe in WKRP in Cincinnati (1978–1982). 

Her performance as Jennifer caught the eye of Hugh Wilson, the show’s creator and director, who was enamored with how Loni Anderson combined the irresistible looks and innocent sexuality of Jayne Mansfield and Marilyn Monroe into one captivating persona.
